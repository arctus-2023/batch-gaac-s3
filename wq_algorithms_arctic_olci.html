Context. James Bay and Hudson Bay are optically complex Case-2 subarctic waters dominated by allochthonous CDOM from large river inputs (Nelson, Hayes, Nottaway, La Grande rivers), high mineral SPM from permafrost/glacial erosion, and moderate seasonal phytoplankton blooms. Standard open-ocean algorithms (OC4, GSM, GIOP) systematically fail under these conditions. All algorithms below are evaluated for applicability to this optical regime.

Arctic challenge summary. CDOM absorption dominates 400–560 nm → blue-band Chla algorithms overestimate 2–10×; high SPM saturates red bands → switching NIR algorithm needed; low solar angles in arctic summer → Polymer AC preferred over Black Pixel Assumption.

§2

CDOM — Colored Dissolved Organic Matter

Target variable: absorption coefficient aCDOM(412) or aCDOM(443) [m⁻¹], or dissolved organic carbon (DOC) [mg L⁻¹] derived therefrom. Arctic/subarctic rivers export massive allochthonous DOC. CDOM dominates absorption below 490 nm and renders blue-band Chla algorithms unreliable. Band ratios at 412–490 nm are the most diagnostic.
1
Matsuoka et al. (2013) — Semi-Analytical GIOP | Southern Beaufort Sea
Semi-analytical GIOP inversion with regional parametrisation  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Formula
a(λ) = a_w(λ) + a_ph(λ) + a_CDOM(λ) + a_NAP(λ) a_NAP(443) = α · bbp(443)^β [NAP separated from CDOM] a_CDOM(443) = a_CDM(443) − a_NAP(443) a_CDOM(λ) = a_CDOM(443) · exp[−S · (λ − 443)], S ≈ 0.018 nm⁻¹
Coefficients α, β fitted from Southern Beaufort Sea field data.
OLCI bands Oa02 (412.5), Oa03 (442.5), Oa04 (490), Oa05 (510), Oa06 (560), Oa08 (665) nm
Output range aCDOM(443) = 0.01–1.5 m⁻¹; DOC: 55 + 357 × aCDOM(443) [µM]
Uncertainty ~35% oceanic waters; ~50% coastal river-dominated zones
Reference Matsuoka, A. et al. (2013). Biogeosciences, 10, 917–927. doi:10.5194/bg-10-917-2013
Arctic notes Developed specifically for the Mackenzie River plume / southern Beaufort Sea. The co-derived DOC formula (Matsuoka et al., 2012) is widely cited for Canadian Arctic shelf waters. Best proxy available for Hudson Bay open-shelf CDOM; may underperform in very shallow nearshore James Bay river-plume pixels.
2
Glukhovets et al. (2020) — Empirical Band-Ratio | Arctic Seas (OLCI)
Empirical log-linear regression on OLCI Rrs ratio  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Formula
log₁₀[a_CDOM(443)] = a₀ + a₁ · log₁₀[Rrs(442.5) / Rrs(490)]
Coefficients a₀, a₁ fitted from Russian Arctic expedition data (White Sea, Barents, Kara, Laptev). Alternative ratio Rrs(412)/Rrs(490) also evaluated.
OLCI bands Oa03 (442.5) / Oa04 (490) nm — primary; Oa02 (412.5) optional
Output range aCDOM(443) ≈ 0.03–1.2 m⁻¹
Uncertainty 40–80% for uncalibrated algorithms; regional re-fit significantly reduces errors
Reference Glukhovets, D. et al. (2020). Remote Sensing, 12(19), 3210. doi:10.3390/rs12193210
Arctic notes First study presenting actual Sentinel-3 OLCI CDOM uncertainties across Russian Arctic seas. Evaluated five algorithms in direct OLCI matchups; the 442/490 ratio consistently outperformed all others in high-terrigenous-CDOM Arctic shelf waters. Directly applicable to James Bay if local coefficients are re-fitted.
3
El Kassar / Juhls et al. (2023) — OLCI DOC/CDOM | Lena River / Laptev Sea
Empirical multi-band regression after Polymer AC  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Formula
ln[a_CDOM(412)] = b₀ + b₁·ln[Rrs(412)/Rrs(443)] + b₂·ln[Rrs(412)/Rrs(490)] DOC [mg L⁻¹] = c₀ + c₁ · a_CDOM(412) [river-specific DOC–aCDOM regression]
Rrs from Polymer atmospheric correction (critical for arctic low-sun-angle conditions).
OLCI bands Oa02 (412.5), Oa03 (442.5), Oa04 (490) nm
Performance MAPD = 10.89%, RMSE = 1.55 mg L⁻¹, r² = 0.92 (n = 489 matchups, 2018–2021)
Reference El Kassar, J., Juhls, B. et al. (2023). Frontiers in Marine Science, 10, 1082109. doi:10.3389/fmars.2023.1082109
Arctic notes Validated in Siberian Arctic river delta (Lena/Laptev) — the methodological blueprint most transferable to James Bay/Hudson Bay river-plume CDOM monitoring. Uses Polymer AC specifically to handle low sun angles. Dense field campaign validation (489 matchups) is the largest for Arctic river-influenced OLCI work to date.
4
Lewis & Arrigo (2020) — AO.GSM / AOReg.emp | Pan-Arctic
Arctic-optimized GSM semi-analytical + empirical polynomial  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★
Formula
log₁₀[a_CDOM(443)] = A₀ + A₁·X + A₂·X² + A₃·X³ + A₄·X⁴ X = log₁₀[ max(Rrs(443), Rrs(490), Rrs(510)) / Rrs(560) ]
Polynomial coefficients re-fitted from the largest pan-Arctic in situ bio-optical dataset assembled to date (replaces standard OC4 global coefficients).
OLCI bands Oa03 (442.5), Oa04 (490), Oa05 (510), Oa06 (560) nm
Output range aCDOM(443) = 0.005–1.5 m⁻¹; Chla simultaneously (see §3)
Reference Lewis, K.M. & Arrigo, K.R. (2020). Journal of Geophysical Research: Oceans, 125, e2019JC015706. doi:10.1029/2019JC015706
Arctic notes Purpose-built for the full Arctic Ocean using pan-Arctic in situ dataset. Demonstrates OC4/GSM overestimate CDOM in Arctic. Best suited for open Hudson Bay / offshore shelf; blue bands still affected in most CDOM-rich nearshore James Bay river plumes. Excellent pan-Arctic benchmark.
5
Mabit, Araújo, Singh & Bélanger (2022) — Québec Coastal / James Bay Eastern Shore
Empirical power-law regression (blue/green ratio)  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Formula
a_CDOM(443) = α · [Rrs(443) / Rrs(560)]^β
Coefficients α, β calibrated from field campaigns along the lower St. Lawrence estuary and eastern James Bay coast. Secondary predictor: Rrs(412)/Rrs(560).
OLCI bands Oa03 (442.5), Oa06 (560) nm — primary; Oa02 (412.5) secondary
Output range aCDOM(443) ≈ 0.1–3.5 m⁻¹ (covers highly CDOM-dominated estuarine plumes)
Reference Mabit, R. et al. (2022). Frontiers in Remote Sensing, 3, 834908. doi:10.3389/frsen.2022.834908
Arctic notes The only published WQ algorithm specifically calibrated with eastern James Bay + St. Lawrence estuary in situ data. SPM algorithm from the same study (see §4) ensures a consistent multi-parameter framework. Primary validation was Landsat-8 OLI / Sentinel-2 MSI but band definitions (443, 560 nm) match OLCI Oa03/Oa06 directly; minor recalibration recommended.
§3

Chlorophyll-a (Chla)

CDOM bias warning. Standard blue-green band-ratio algorithms (OC3, OC4, OC4Me) overestimate Chla by 2–10× in James Bay/Hudson Bay because CDOM co-absorbs at 443–490 nm, mimicking phytoplankton. NIR-red algorithms (665/709 nm) that operate where CDOM absorption is negligible are strongly preferred for nearshore, river-plume, and eutrophic subarctic pixels.
1
OC4Me — ESA/EUMETSAT Operational Sentinel-3 Algorithm
Empirical maximum band-ratio polynomial  |  Arctic applicability: ★★ (reference baseline only)
Formula
log₁₀(Chla) = A₀ + A₁·X + A₂·X² + A₃·X³ + A₄·X⁴ X = log₁₀[ max(Rrs(442.5), Rrs(490), Rrs(510)) / Rrs(560) ] OLCI coefficients: A₀=0.4503, A₁=−3.2595, A₂=3.5227, A₃=−3.3594, A₄=0.9496
OLCI bands Oa03 (442.5), Oa04 (490), Oa05 (510), Oa06 (560) nm
Output range Chla ≈ 0.03–30 mg m⁻³ (Case-1 waters)
Reference ESA OLCI L2 ATBD (2013). PUG-ATBD-0012. Based on O'Reilly et al. (1998) J. Geophys. Res., 103(C11), 24937. doi:10.1029/98JC02160
Arctic notes ESA standard L2 OL_2_WFR product. Systematically overestimates Chla 2–10× in CDOM-rich arctic waters (Lewis & Arrigo 2020). Use as reference product for comparison only; do not rely on it for primary Chla retrieval in James Bay nearshore areas. Useful for offshore open Hudson Bay.
2
Gons et al. (2005) — Semi-Analytical NIR-Red (CDOM-Insensitive)
Semi-analytical 2-band NIR-red model  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Formula
Chla = [ (Rrs(708) / Rrs(665)) · a_w(665) − a_w(708) + b_bp ] / a_ph*(665) where: a_w(665) ≈ 0.401 m⁻¹ (pure water absorption at 665 nm) a_w(708) ≈ 0.703 m⁻¹ (pure water absorption at 708.75 nm) b_bp = 1.61 · Rrs(779) / (0.082 − 0.6 · Rrs(779)) [from Oa16] a_ph*(665) ≈ 0.0149 m² mg⁻¹ (chlorophyll-specific absorption)
OLCI bands Oa08 (665), Oa11 (708.75), Oa16 (778.75) nm
Output range Chla ≈ 3–185 mg m⁻³; robust in turbid, CDOM-rich, eutrophic waters
Reference Gons, H.J., Rijkeboer, M., Ruddick, K.G. (2005). Journal of Plankton Research, 27(2), 125–133. doi:10.1093/plankt/fbh151
Arctic notes Operates entirely in the red/NIR where CDOM absorption is negligible → immune to the CDOM contamination problem of blue-band algorithms. Extensively validated on MERIS (same bands as OLCI). Binding et al. (2011) applied Gons-type NIR-red to Great Lakes and Hudson Bay coastal MERIS data with good results. Recommended first choice for CDOM-rich nearshore James Bay Chla retrieval.
3
NDCI — Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index (Mishra & Mishra, 2012)
Empirical normalized difference index  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★
Formula
NDCI = (Rrs(708) − Rrs(665)) / (Rrs(708) + Rrs(665)) Chla = 14.039 + 86.115 · NDCI + 194.325 · NDCI² [original calibration, r²=0.95] (regional polynomial re-fit recommended for arctic waters)
OLCI bands Oa08 (665), Oa11 (708.75) nm
Output range Chla ≈ 1–150 mg m⁻³; turbid productive estuaries
Reference Mishra, S. & Mishra, D.R. (2012). Remote Sensing of Environment, 117, 394–406. doi:10.1016/j.rse.2011.10.016
Arctic notes CDOM-insensitive (665 and 708 nm only). Particularly relevant for James Bay spring blooms and estuarine nutrient-rich plumes. Available as a direct SNAP product for OLCI. Calibration coefficients need regional re-fitting — cold arctic water temperatures affect aph*(665).
4
Lewis & Arrigo (2020) — Arctic-Optimized OC4 Polynomial (AOReg.emp)
Empirical MBR polynomial re-fitted to pan-Arctic in situ data  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★
Formula
log₁₀(Chla) = A₀ + A₁·X + A₂·X² + A₃·X³ + A₄·X⁴ X = log₁₀[ max(Rrs(443), Rrs(490), Rrs(510)) / Rrs(560) ] Arctic coefficients (approx.): A₀=0.3255, A₁=−2.7677, A₂=1.4927, A₃=−1.2082, A₄=−0.5966
OLCI bands Oa03 (442.5), Oa04 (490), Oa05 (510), Oa06 (560) nm
Output range Chla ≈ 0.05–15 mg m⁻³; pan-Arctic open shelf
Reference Lewis, K.M. & Arrigo, K.R. (2020). Journal of Geophysical Research: Oceans, 125, e2019JC015706. doi:10.1029/2019JC015706
Arctic notes Reduces CDOM bias by re-fitting to Arctic bio-optical training data. Still uses blue bands → may overestimate in the most CDOM-rich nearshore James Bay pixels. Best for open Hudson Bay / offshore shelf where CDOM loads are moderate. Provides a consistent CDOM + Chla retrieval from a single algorithm framework.
5
FLH — Fluorescence Line Height (Letelier & Abbott 1996 / OLCI)
Semi-analytical baseline-subtracted fluorescence index  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★
Formula
FLH = L_w(681.25) − 0.5 · [L_w(665) + L_w(708.75)] Chla ∝ FLH / [PAR · a_ph*(681) · (1 − ω_p)] [quasi-analytical form]
Linear baseline drawn between Oa08 and Oa11; Oa10 (681.25 nm) captures the Chla fluorescence peak.
OLCI bands Oa08 (665), Oa10 (681.25), Oa11 (708.75) nm
Output range Chla ≈ 0.05–50 mg m⁻³; most reliable at moderate concentrations (1–30 mg m⁻³)
Reference Letelier, R.M. & Abbott, M.R. (1996). Geophysical Research Letters, 23(16), 2111–2114. doi:10.1029/96GL01228
OLCI adaptation: Gower, J.F.R. et al. (2004). MCI described in ESA OLCI ATBD.
Arctic notes CDOM-insensitive (all bands in red). Useful for detecting Chla spatial patterns (bloom mapping) but less accurate for absolute concentration due to photoacclimation effects in arctic phytoplankton, variable quantum yield at low temperatures, and NPQ quenching. Best used as a qualitative bloom indicator or in multi-algorithm ensemble.
6
Pahlevan et al. (2020) — MDN Machine-Learning for OLCI / MSI
Mixture Density Network (MDN) ensemble ML, all OLCI visible bands  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★
Formula Input: [Rrs(412), Rrs(443), Rrs(490), Rrs(510), Rrs(560), Rrs(665), Rrs(674), Rrs(681), Rrs(709), Rrs(754), Rrs(779), Rrs(865)] → MDN → Chla + uncertainty
OLCI bands Oa02–Oa12, Oa16, Oa17 (full spectrum 412–865 nm)
Performance Significantly outperforms OC4Me across Case-2 inland and coastal waters; r² ≈ 0.79 in high-latitude waters
Reference Pahlevan, N. et al. (2020). Remote Sensing of Environment, 240, 111604. doi:10.1016/j.rse.2020.111604
Arctic notes ML approaches trained on large mixed Case-2 datasets better capture the nonlinear optical complexity of CDOM+SPM-dominated northern waters than any single empirical formula. Main risk: model performance depends on training data representativeness — models without James Bay / Hudson Bay samples may carry biases. The MDN also outputs per-pixel uncertainty estimates (useful for QA/QC).
§4

SPM / TSM — Suspended Particulate Matter

Target variable: total suspended matter SPM or TSM [g m⁻³]. James Bay SPM is dominated by fine-grained mineral sediment (clay/silt) from the Nelson, Hayes, and Nottaway rivers. Concentrations span <0.5 g m⁻³ (open offshore) to >100 g m⁻³ (near river mouths). This large dynamic range demands a switching scheme between the 665 nm red band (moderate turbidity) and the 865 nm NIR band (high turbidity, when the red saturates).
1
Nechad, Ruddick & Park (2010) — Generic Single-Band Semi-Empirical
Semi-empirical single-band (665 or 865 nm)  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★
Formula
SPM [g m⁻³] = (A_SPM · ρw(λ)) / (1 − ρw(λ)/C_SPM) + B_SPM where ρw(λ) = π · Rrs(λ) Published OLCI band coefficients: λ=665 nm (Oa08): A=355.85, C=0.1728, B=1.74 λ=708 nm (Oa11): A=496.52, C=0.2112, B=1.74 λ=865 nm (Oa17): A=3077.2, C=0.2112, B=−2.40
OLCI bands Oa08 (665) primary; Oa11 (708.75) transitional; Oa17 (865) high turbidity
Output range SPM ≈ 0.5–1000 g m⁻³ (with switching between bands)
Reference Nechad, B., Ruddick, K.G., Park, Y. (2010). Remote Sensing of Environment, 114(4), 854–866. doi:10.1016/j.rse.2009.11.022
Updated LUT: Nechad, B. et al. (2016). Earth System Science Data, 8, 173–196. doi:10.5194/essd-8-173-2016
Arctic notes Semi-analytical form gives moderate cross-region generalizability (~27–29% uncertainty without recalibration). The 708 nm band (Oa11) — available on OLCI/MERIS but not MODIS/Landsat — is particularly useful for 10–100 g m⁻³ range typical of subarctic river plumes. Re-fitting A and C using local James Bay in situ SPM vs. Rrs strongly recommended.
2
Dogliotti et al. (2015) — Switching Algorithm (Red + NIR)
Semi-empirical switching with linear blend zone  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★
Formula
Low turbidity (T < 7 FNU): use λ_red ≈ 665 nm (Oa08) T [FNU] = (228.1 · ρw(665)) / (1 − ρw(665)/0.1641) High turbidity (T > 20 FNU): use λ_NIR = 865 nm (Oa17) T [FNU] = (3078.9 · ρw(865)) / (1 − ρw(865)/0.2112) Blend zone 7–20 FNU: linear interpolation between branches SPM [g m⁻³] ≈ 1.24 · T^1.12 [empirical T→SPM conversion]
Note: original calibration used 645 nm (not in OLCI); Oa08 (665 nm) with recalibration recommended.
OLCI bands Oa08 (665) + Oa17 (865) nm
Output range T ≈ 0.5–700 FNU; SPM ≈ 0.5–1000 g m⁻³
Reference Dogliotti, A.I. et al. (2015). Remote Sensing of Environment, 156, 157–168. doi:10.1016/j.rse.2014.09.020
Arctic notes The switching scheme handles the large dynamic range of James Bay/Hudson Bay (clear offshore → highly turbid near-shore). The asymptotic C parameter is theoretically linked to particle scattering and is moderately stable across sediment types. For arctic mineral particles (fine clay from permafrost erosion) recalibration of A is advisable. Used in the ESA OLCI operational ATBD for turbid waters.
3
Doxaran et al. (2012) — Band-Ratio SPM | Mackenzie River Arctic Plume
Empirical power-law NIR/VIS ratio  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Formula
SPM [g m⁻³] = A · [Rrs(λ_NIR) / Rrs(λ_VIS)]^B Mackenzie plume calibration (MODIS-equivalent, 2012): Rrs(859) / Rrs(555): A≈1306, B≈1.28 Rrs(859) / Rrs(645): A≈820, B≈1.10 [for very turbid pixels] OLCI equivalent bands: Rrs(Oa17=865) / Rrs(Oa06=560) or Rrs(Oa17=865) / Rrs(Oa08=665)
OLCI bands Oa06 (560) or Oa08 (665) denominator; Oa17 (865) numerator
Output range SPM ≈ 1–2000 g m⁻³ (validated in highly turbid Mackenzie delta)
Reference Doxaran, D., Devred, E., Babin, M. (2012). Biogeosciences, 9, 3213–3229. doi:10.5194/bg-9-3213-2012
Arctic notes Calibrated on Mackenzie River particles entering the Beaufort Sea — arctic clay/silt sediment from permafrost erosion, the most analogous published calibration dataset to Nelson/Hayes rivers entering Hudson Bay. NIR/VIS ratio is stable across illumination and less sensitive to CDOM. Highly applicable to James Bay river-mouth plumes.
4
Mabit & Bélanger (2022) — St. Lawrence / Eastern James Bay SPM
Empirical regional regression (NIR band)  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Formula
SPM [g m⁻³] = A · Rrs(λ)^B or A · Rrs(λ) / (1 − Rrs(λ)/C) [Band Oa08=665 or Oa17=865 nm depending on SPM range] See Mabit et al. (2022) Table 2 for regional coefficients calibrated with James Bay eastern coast field data.
OLCI bands Oa06 (560), Oa08 (665), Oa17 (865) nm
Output range Subarctic estuarine range; covers eastern James Bay gradients
Reference Mabit, R. et al. (2022). Frontiers in Remote Sensing, 3, 834908. doi:10.3389/frsen.2022.834908
Arctic notes Directly calibrated with James Bay eastern shore in situ SPM data — the most site-specific algorithm available. Part of a consistent CDOM + SPM framework from the same study. Band definitions compatible with OLCI Oa06/Oa08/Oa17; minor sensor inter-calibration offset may apply.
5
Subirade et al. (2024) — OLCI SPM Algorithm Intercomparison (Best Performers)
Comprehensive evaluation of 12 OLCI SPM algorithms  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★
Key finding Among 12 algorithms evaluated (Nechad10/16, GIOP, QAA, C2RCC, MDN, SOLID), the MDN (machine learning) and SOLID (semi-analytical bbp-based) outperformed all single-band empirical methods across contrasted coastal waters. Best errors: ~50% MAPD, slope ≈ 1.06.
SOLID formula
b_bp(λ) → SPM via: SPM = γ · b_bp(λ)^δ b_bp from multi-band IOP inversion using Oa04,Oa06,Oa08,Oa11,Oa12,Oa16,Oa17
OLCI bands Oa04 (490), Oa06 (560), Oa08 (665), Oa11 (708.75), Oa12 (753.75), Oa16 (778.75), Oa17 (865) nm
Reference Subirade, C. et al. (2024). Optics Express, 32. DOI forthcoming in print. Figshare collection
Arctic notes Most up-to-date (2024) OLCI-specific SPM benchmarking. Validation dataset does not specifically include arctic waters. Recommendation: use MDN or SOLID as the baseline modern approach, benchmarked against Nechad10 single-band with local recalibration for James Bay.
§5

Turbidity

Target variable: Turbidity [FNU or NTU]. Turbidity integrates both mineral and organic particle scattering; the SPM-to-turbidity ratio varies with particle type (high for mineral-dominated arctic sediments). A switching red/NIR scheme is essential for the full James Bay dynamic range. Key note: OLCI lacks a 645 nm band; Oa07 (620 nm) or Oa08 (665 nm) serves as the red substitute.
1
Dogliotti et al. (2015) — Single Switching Turbidity Algorithm
Semi-empirical switching (red + NIR) — also returns turbidity directly in FNU  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★
Formula
T [FNU] = (A_T · ρw(λ)) / (1 − ρw(λ)/C_T) Low turbidity (λ=645 nm → OLCI: use Oa08=665 nm): A_T=228.1, C_T=0.1641 High turbidity (λ=859 nm → OLCI: Oa17=865 nm): A_T=3078.9, C_T=0.2112 Blend zone 7–20 FNU: linear interpolation
OLCI bands Oa08 (665) + Oa17 (865) nm (with recalibration from original 645 nm)
Output range T ≈ 0.5–700 FNU
Reference Dogliotti, A.I. et al. (2015). Remote Sensing of Environment, 156, 157–168. doi:10.1016/j.rse.2014.09.020
Arctic notes Gold standard switching turbidity algorithm; validated across Rio de la Plata, Belgian coast, and San Francisco Bay. Self-switching eliminates NIR-saturation issues. For arctic mineral particles with unique single-scattering albedo, recalibration of A_T using local in situ FNU data will substantially improve accuracy.
2
Nechad et al. (2016) — OLCI-Specific Turbidity LUT
Semi-empirical per-band LUT calibrated to OLCI wavelengths  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★
Formula
T [FNU] = (A_T · ρw(λ)) / (1 − ρw(λ)/C_T) + B_T OLCI-specific coefficients (Nechad 2016 LUT): Oa08 (665 nm): A_T=188.49, C_T=0.164, B_T≈0 Oa11 (708.75 nm): A_T=261.7, C_T=0.212, B_T≈0 Oa16 (778.75 nm): A_T=1414, C_T=0.212, B_T≈0 Oa17 (865 nm): A_T=3265, C_T=0.212, B_T≈0
OLCI bands Oa08 (665), Oa11 (708.75), Oa16 (778.75), Oa17 (865) nm
Output range T ≈ 0.5–500 FNU per band (multi-band switching available)
Reference Nechad, B. et al. (2016). Earth System Science Data, 8, 173–196. doi:10.5194/essd-8-173-2016
Arctic notes The OLCI-specific coefficient LUT eliminates band-mismatch errors. The Oa11 band (708.75 nm) fills the transitional 5–50 FNU range especially useful for subarctic coastal river plumes. Use in a multi-band switching scheme: Oa08 → Oa11 → Oa17 with increasing turbidity thresholds.
3
ESA OLCI Operational Turbid Water Turbidity (L2 ATBD)
Operational ESA blended switching algorithm embedded in OL_2_WFR product  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★
Formula
Switching scheme (adapted Dogliotti 2015 for OLCI): T < ~7 FNU: Oa08 = 665 nm branch 7–20 FNU: Oa11 = 708.75 nm blended T > 20 FNU: Oa17 = 865 nm branch Output as TUR variable in ESA Sentinel-3 OL_2_WFR product.
OLCI bands Oa08 (665), Oa11 (708.75), Oa17 (865) nm
Reference ESA OLCI L2 Turbid Water ATBD. SENTINEL-3 L2 Products and Algorithm Definition. ESA STEP document
Arctic notes Standard baseline product. Global calibration; significant biases expected for arctic mineral-particle sediments. Use as a quick-look reference; supplement with regionally calibrated algorithms. The TUR variable is directly available in the ESA OL_2_WFR NetCDF4 product.

Processor-Level (Combined) Algorithms

These processors simultaneously perform atmospheric correction and retrieve multiple water quality products (CDOM, Chla, SPM) from the full Rrs spectrum. They offer operational convenience but require evaluation against local in situ data for arctic/subarctic conditions.
P1
C2RCC / C2X — Case 2 Regional Coast Color Neural Network (Brockmann et al., 2016)
Two-stage neural network (AC + IOP inversion)  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★ (C2X: ★★★★)
Architecture Stage 1 NN: TOA radiance → Rrs(λ) for all OLCI bands
Stage 2 NN: Rrs(λ) → IOPs [adg(443), aph(443), bbp(443)]
Concentrations: Chla = aph(443) / aph*; TSM = bbp / bsp; CDOM ≈ adg(443)
OLCI bands Oa02–Oa11 (412.5–708.75 nm); full visible spectrum used
Output range C2RCC: Chla 0.01–200 mg m⁻³, TSM 0.1–500 g m⁻³, CDOM 0.001–5 m⁻¹
C2X (extreme waters): Chla to 200, TSM to 1000 g m⁻³, CDOM to 20 m⁻¹
Reference Brockmann, C. et al. (2016). ESA Living Planet Symposium 2016, ESA-SP 740. Available in SNAP toolbox.
Arctic notes ESA standard Case-2 product for OLCI L2. C2X (extended training range) is recommended for highly turbid James Bay river plumes. Training data do not specifically include Arctic bio-optics; CDOM retrievals in subarctic river-plume waters may carry systematic biases (Glukhovets et al. 2020). Operationally convenient: simultaneous multi-parameter retrieval in SNAP. Use as first-cut baseline before applying parameter-specific algorithms.
P2
ONNS — OLCI Neural Network Swarm (Hieronymi, Müller & Doerffer, 2017)
Ensemble of 13 optical-water-type–specific NNs with automatic blending  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★
Architecture 1. OWT classification of Rrs(λ) into 1 of 13 optical water types
2. OWT-specific NN applied for IOP retrieval
3. IOPs → Chla, ISM (inorganic SPM), aCDOM(440), Kd(490), uncertainty
OLCI bands Oa01–Oa12 (400–753.75 nm); full visible spectrum
Output range Chla 0.01–200 mg m⁻³, ISM 0.01–1500 g m⁻³, aCDOM(440) 0.0001–20 m⁻¹
Reference Hieronymi, M., Müller, D., Doerffer, R. (2017). Frontiers in Marine Science, 4, 140. doi:10.3389/fmars.2017.00140
Arctic notes The OWT classification explicitly handles optical diversity of mixed waters. OWT classes 12–13 (extreme absorbing) are designed for "optically black" high-CDOM waters typical near James Bay river mouths. ISM range to 1500 g m⁻³ covers extreme arctic sediment events. OWT-based blending provides better performance than a single-NN in optically variable subarctic scenes. No specific Arctic validation reported, but framework inherently captures high-CDOM classes.
P3
Polymer — Atmospheric Correction for OLCI (Steinmetz & Ramon, 2018)
Coupled ocean-atmosphere spectral polynomial AC — produces Rrs for all in-water algorithms  |  Arctic applicability: ★★★★★
Key principle
ρ_TOA(λ) = ρ_atm(λ) + TT · ρ_w(λ) + ρ_glint(λ) Atmospheric + glint terms modelled as polynomial in λ; water term uses forward bio-optical model. All parameters fitted simultaneously by spectral cost function minimisation.
Output Rrs(λ) for all OLCI bands → feeds any in-water algorithm above
Reference Steinmetz, F., Ramon, D. (2018). SPIE Proc., 10778, 1077810. doi:10.1117/12.2500232
Original: Steinmetz, F. et al. (2011). Optics Express, 19, 9783–9800.
Arctic notes Strongly recommended AC step for all James Bay / Hudson Bay OLCI processing.
Advantages over ESA standard AC (Black Pixel Assumption):
  • Handles high sun-zenith angles (>60°) in arctic summer
  • Explicit sun-glint removal across wide azimuth range
  • Works in turbid nearshore waters where NIR dark-pixel assumption fails
  • Used successfully by El Kassar/Juhls et al. (2023) for Lena River OLCI CDOM retrieval

Recommendations for James Bay / Hudson Bay Pipeline

Atmospheric Correction

  • Polymer (Steinmetz & Ramon 2018) — handles high SZA, sun-glint, turbid nearshore
  • ACOLITE acceptable alternative for very turbid nearshore pixels (already in pipeline)
  • Avoid ESA standard Black Pixel AC for high-CDOM / arctic SZA conditions

CDOM

  • Mabit & Bélanger (2022) — only James Bay–specific calibration available
  • Matsuoka et al. (2013) — for open Hudson Bay shelf (Beaufort Sea analogue)
  • El Kassar/Juhls (2023) framework — methodological blueprint for OLCI river-plume CDOM
  • Avoid blue-band GSM/QAA in highly CDOM-dominated nearshore pixels

Chlorophyll-a

  • Gons NIR-red (2005) — CDOM-insensitive; first choice for nearshore plumes
  • NDCI (Mishra 2012) — for spring bloom / productive periods
  • Lewis & Arrigo AOReg — for open offshore Hudson Bay (lower CDOM)
  • Do NOT use OC4Me in river-plume / nearshore zones

SPM / TSM

  • Doxaran et al. (2012) — Mackenzie Arctic plume calibration, most analogous mineralogy
  • Nechad (2010) + switching scheme — Oa08 (low) → Oa11 (mid) → Oa17 (high turbidity)
  • Mabit & Bélanger (2022) — if local James Bay in situ SPM data available for recalibration

Turbidity

  • Dogliotti et al. (2015) — gold standard switching algorithm
  • Nechad (2016) OLCI LUT — use Oa11 (708 nm) for intermediate turbidity range
  • Recalibrate AT with local in situ FNU measurements for arctic mineral particles

Combined / Operational

  • C2X — operational multi-parameter retrieval (SNAP); use for quick-look runs
  • ONNS — preferred over C2RCC for optically diverse subarctic scenes (13 OWT classes)
  • Both should be validated against parameter-specific algorithms above
Key caution for James Bay. All algorithms except Mabit & Bélanger (2022) and Doxaran et al. (2012) were calibrated outside James Bay and Hudson Bay. Regional recalibration (even if only adjusting the leading coefficient A) using in situ measurements from the Nelson/Hayes/La Grande river plumes will substantially improve retrieval accuracy. Concurrent field campaigns with Sentinel-3 overpasses are strongly recommended as a validation strategy.
